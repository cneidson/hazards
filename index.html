Storm Risk and Environmental Hazards in NYC

New York City is vulnerable to coastal storms, including nor'easters, tropical storms and hurricanes. Residents living in floodprone areas face risks not only from coastal storms themselves, but also from long-term environmental and public health hazards associated with flooding in these areas. Much of this is linked to the long history of manufacturing and industrial enterprises in New York.

These risks will be explored in this map.

What are evacuation zones?

As part of its diaster managment planning, New York City has designated six hurricane evacuation zones to identify areas likely to be inundated by storm surge. In the event of a hurricane or tropical storm, residents in the hurricane zones may be ordered to evacuate.

There are six levels of evacuation zones. Zone 1 has the highest risk and is the most likely to flood.

These zones were unveiled by the city in 2013 to expand on older hurricane evacuation zones and help the city better communicate with affected communities. The revised zones drew in an additional 600,000 New Yorkers not included within the boundaries of the former zones.

What is the impact of storm flooding?

For many New Yokers, the city's storm vulnerability was made very apparent when Hurricane Sandy hit the city on October 29, 2012. The storm resulted in 44 deaths, and an estimated $19 billion in damages and economic loses. More than 69,000 residential unit were damaged, and thousands of residents were temporarily displaced.
